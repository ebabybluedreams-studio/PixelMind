PROMPT 01
The Mood-to-Palette Prompt
▸ COPY THIS — FILL IN THE BRACKETS — PASTE INTO ANY AI TOOL 
I'm designing for [describe the brand or project in 1-2 sentences].

The emotional atmosphere I want to create is:
[describe the feeling — e.g., "quiet luxury with an undercurrent of danger,"
"nostalgic but not sentimental," "rebellious energy that still feels high-end"].

The audience this needs to speak to:
[describe as a real person, not a demographic].

Build me a complete 5-color palette:
  — Name each color (a real name, not just a color description)
  — Give me the exact hex code
  — Assign a role: Primary / Secondary / Accent / Neutral / Base
  — Tell me the emotion this specific color carries
  — Tell me exactly where and how to use it

Then write 2-3 sentences explaining the creative logic behind this palette —
why these colors work together and what the palette communicates before a word is read.
WHY THIS WORKS

Every palette tool gives you colors. This gives you a color SYSTEM — with roles, emotions, and a rationale you can present to a client as strategy, not preference.

WHAT YOU GET

A 5-color system with names, hex codes, roles, usage guide, and creative director rationale. Client-ready from the first output.

PROMPT 02
The Typography Brief Prompt
▸ COPY THIS — FILL IN THE BRACKETS — PASTE INTO ANY AI TOOL 
My brand/project: [describe in 2-3 sentences — what it does, who it's for,
what it should feel like to encounter it].

What this brand should NOT feel like: [name the aesthetic you're avoiding].

Find me 3 font pairings. For each give me:

  DISPLAY FONT: [exact name — Google Fonts preferred]
  Why this display font: [the specific psychological reason, not just "it's elegant"]

  BODY FONT: [exact name]
  Why this body font: [why it pairs with the display, what it adds]

  The creative tension: [what makes this combination interesting vs. just safe]
  Personality tag: [2-3 words]
  Best use case: [1 sentence]

Make each pairing genuinely distinct — not 3 variations of the same personality.
WHY THIS WORKS

Most font guides give you safe pairings. Safe is forgettable. This prompt extracts the REASONING — so you can explain your choices like a senior typographer instead of "it just feels right."

WHAT YOU GET

3 distinct, defensible font pairings with the reasoning already written. Present to a client with confidence.

PROMPT 03
The Brief Builder Prompt
▸ COPY THIS — FILL IN THE BRACKETS — PASTE INTO ANY AI TOOL 
Build a complete professional design brief for:

Project type: [e.g., brand identity, website redesign, packaging, campaign]
Client/brand: [name]
Context: [budget, existing brand elements, specific constraints]

Write it so a designer who knows nothing about this project could start tomorrow.
Include:

  PROJECT OVERVIEW: [2-3 sentences, business context + what this needs to accomplish]
  OBJECTIVES: [3 specific, measurable objectives]
  TARGET AUDIENCE: [real human description — their taste, what they respond to]
  TONE & VOICE: [describe as a person, not an adjective list]
  DELIVERABLES: [specific list with format notes]
  CONSTRAINTS: [what NOT to do — this section prevents scope creep]
  INSPIRATION DIRECTION: [specific aesthetics, designers, brands, eras]
  WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE: [how does it feel when this is working?]
  TIMELINE: [phases with dates if possible]
WHY THIS WORKS

Scope creep doesn't start at revision 4. It starts when there's no brief. This prompt creates the document that protects your time and your rate before a single pixel is placed.

WHAT YOU GET

A complete, professional brief ready to send before work starts. Use it to align expectations and stop scope creep before it begins.

PROMPT 04
The Design Critic Prompt
▸ COPY THIS — FILL IN THE BRACKETS — PASTE INTO ANY AI TOOL 
Act as a senior art director — 20+ years of experience, strong opinions,
someone who will tell me the truth without sugarcoating. Be specific.
Vague feedback is useless.

Here's the design: [describe in detail — layout, colors, typography, imagery,
spacing, hierarchy, what it's for, who it's for, what it should make people feel]

Give me:

  1. SCORE: A number out of 100. One sentence explaining it.

  2. STRENGTHS (3 specific): Exactly what works and why it matters.

  3. PROBLEMS (3 specific): Name the exact issue. Why it hurts the design.
     Then give a concrete fix — not "improve the hierarchy" but the specific change.

  4. QUICK WINS (3): Things I can implement in under 30 minutes.

  5. THE MOVE THAT MAKES IT ICONIC: One bold choice that elevates this from
     solid to unforgettable. Don't play it safe.
WHY THIS WORKS

You cannot objectively critique your own work. You're too close. This prompt gives you a structured external perspective on demand — specific enough to act on immediately.

WHAT YOU GET

A scored critique with fixes attached to every problem. Use it before client delivery or any time you need a real perspective instead of polite feedback.

PROMPT 05
The Client Language Translator
▸ COPY THIS — FILL IN THE BRACKETS — PASTE INTO ANY AI TOOL 
My client gave me this feedback: [paste their exact words]

Translate their feedback into specific, actionable design direction.

  1. WHAT THEY'RE ACTUALLY REACTING TO: Identify the real design element —
     contrast? Hierarchy? Color temperature? Typographic weight?

  2. WHAT THEY WANT INSTEAD: Based on their language, what outcome are they
     describing even without the words for it?

  3. THREE CONCRETE CHANGES: Specific adjustments that address the real concern.
     Each one implementable today.

  4. WHAT TO SAY BACK TO THEM: 2-3 sentences to confirm direction before
     starting revisions so you're both aligned.
WHY THIS WORKS

"Make it pop" is not design direction. This prompt decodes client language into actual design decisions — saving you revision cycles every single time.

WHAT YOU GET

Client feedback translated into real direction with specific changes. Fewer revisions. Clearer process. Better outcomes.

PROMPT 06
The Brand Consistency Audit
▸ COPY THIS — FILL IN THE BRACKETS — PASTE INTO ANY AI TOOL 
I need a brand consistency audit for a new piece of work.

Existing brand system:
  — Primary colors: [list with hex codes]
  — Typography: [display and body fonts]
  — Logo style: [describe]
  — Imagery direction: [visual style, subject matter]
  — Brand tone: [describe]
  — Always feels like: [describe]
  — Never feels like: [describe]

New piece I'm reviewing: [describe every element — colors, fonts,
layout, imagery style, copy tone]

Audit for brand consistency:
  1. WHAT'S CONSISTENT: Elements true to the system
  2. WHAT'S DRIFTING: Specific elements breaking consistency and why
  3. HOW TO FIX EACH DRIFT: Concrete corrections for every flagged element
  4. VERDICT: Publishable as-is, or needs work first?
WHY THIS WORKS

Brand drift is a slow erosion — one slightly different shade here, a heavier font weight there. This prompt catches it before the client does.

WHAT YOU GET

A full consistency audit that protects brand integrity, whether you're the original designer or inheriting someone else's system.

PROMPT 07
The Concept Expansion Prompt
▸ COPY THIS — FILL IN THE BRACKETS — PASTE INTO ANY AI TOOL 
I have a design concept I'm developing and need to push it further
or explore real alternatives.

Where I am: [describe your current direction — colors, typography, layout
thinking, imagery approach, emotional intent]

What I need: [choose one]
  A) Push this concept further — what makes it more bold, more itself?
  B) Explore alternatives — show me 5 genuinely different directions
  C) Both

For each alternative direction give me:
  — The emotional register: what feeling does this version lead with?
  — Color approach: the system logic, not specific colors
  — Typography direction: what kind of type and why?
  — The design philosophy in one sentence
  — What it gives up vs. the current direction

Then: which direction has the most potential and why?
WHY THIS WORKS

Every designer hits creative walls. The usual escape is a variation of what you already have. This generates genuine alternatives with a framework for evaluating them.

WHAT YOU GET

5 distinct creative directions instantly. Break through a block, give a client real options, or find the version that's actually better than your first idea.
